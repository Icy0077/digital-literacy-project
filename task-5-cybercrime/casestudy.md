# ✅ Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime in which scammers trick people into sending money or sharing confidential banking details through mobile payment applications. With the increasing use of digital payments in India, students and working professionals have become frequent targets of such scams.

A typical UPI fraud begins when a victim receives a phone call, SMS, or message pretending to be from a bank official, delivery service, or customer support agent. The scammer creates urgency by claiming that the victim must verify their account or receive a refund. The victim is then asked to click a payment link or approve a **collect request** on a UPI app. Believing that they are receiving money, the victim unknowingly authorizes a payment instead. In some cases, fraudsters also ask for OTPs or PINs, which allows them to transfer money directly from the victim’s account.

Students, elderly users, and people unfamiliar with digital payment systems are usually targeted because they may not fully understand how UPI approval requests work. The consequences include immediate financial loss, stress, and difficulty recovering the stolen money.

This case highlights the importance of digital awareness. Users must remember that money is never received by entering a UPI PIN and should always verify payment requests before approving any transaction.
